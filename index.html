Featured Research · April 2026

Agentic AI and Occupational Displacement

A multi-regional task exposure analysis of emerging labor market disruption — 236 occupations across 5 US tech metros, extending Nobel laureate Daron Acemoglu and Pascual Restrepo's task displacement framework to handle agentic AI.

Key Findings

  • 93% of information-work occupations in San Francisco cross our moderate AI displacement threshold by 2030
  • Software engineers rank lower than credit analysts, judges, and regulatory affairs officers — flipping the standard automation narrative
  • Measurable 2–3 year adoption lag between metros: Seattle in 2027 looks like NYC in 2029
  • 17 emerging job categories already showing real hiring traction (~1,500 "AI Reviewer" listings on Indeed) — none require coding
  • Framework validates against AIOE (ρ = 0.84) and Eloundou et al.'s GPT exposure (ρ = 0.72)
  • Stress-tested across a 6× range in S-curve adoption parameter — regional ordering survives all 9 scenario-year combinations
